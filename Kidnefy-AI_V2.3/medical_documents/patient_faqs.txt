1. Q: Is there a cure for Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)?
A: Currently, there is no permanent cure for chronic kidney disease. However, treatments can relieve symptoms, reduce complications, and slow its progression. Finding and treating the underlying cause, like diabetes or high blood pressure, is the best way to protect kidney function. For advanced stages, options include dialysis or a kidney transplant.

2. Q: What is the recommended diet for someone with diabetic kidney disease?
A: A renal diabetic diet needs to control both blood sugar and kidney workload. Key points include:
- Limit sodium to less than 2,000 mg per day to control blood pressure.
- Avoid processed meats, fast food, and canned goods.
- If potassium is high, avoid bananas, tomatoes, and potatoes.
- Monitor portion sizes of carbohydrates.
- Choose high-quality proteins (like fish, egg whites, and chicken) but in controlled amounts as advised by a dietitian.

3. Q: Can I take over-the-counter painkillers if I have kidney problems?
A: You should STRICTLY AVOID nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin), naproxen (Aleve), and high-dose aspirin. These medications reduce blood flow to the kidneys and can cause acute kidney injury or worsen existing CKD. Always consult your nephrologist before taking any new medication, including herbal supplements.

4. Q: How often should I test my kidney function if I have diabetes?
A: Patients with Type 2 diabetes should be screened for kidney disease at the time of diagnosis and at least once a year thereafter. The screening must include two tests: an estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR) blood test and an Albumin-to-Creatinine Ratio (ACR) urine test.

5. Q: What is a safe home blood pressure target for a kidney patient?
A: According to KDIGO guidelines, adults with high blood pressure and CKD should be treated to a target systolic blood pressure of less than 120 mm Hg, provided the target is tolerated safely. This stricter target helps protect against further kidney damage.
